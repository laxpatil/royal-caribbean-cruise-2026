Costa Maya, Mexico

Easy, relaxed port • Perfect for toddlers • Ship docks right at the pier!

☀️ ~30°C / 86°F  ·  Sunny & Humid
7:00 – 8:00 AM

🍳 Breakfast on the Ship

Have a full breakfast on board before disembarking. Feed your toddler well and pack snacks — ports can be unpredictable for meal timing.

🚢 Do This First 🍌 Pack Toddler Snacks
8:30 – 9:30 AM

🏖️ Costa Maya Pier Plaza

The pier itself has a beautiful free pool, splash area, and sandy beach — perfect for toddlers! The Mayan Ruins replica area is photo-worthy and shaded. Stroll slowly, let your little one explore the colorful open-air market stalls.

👶 Toddler Splash Area 🚶 Easy Stroll 🌴 Shaded Areas
9:30 – 11:30 AM

🌊 Mahahual Beach

Just a short taxi ride (~5 min) from the pier is Mahahual — a quaint, relaxed Caribbean beach village. The water is calm, shallow, and crystal clear — ideal for a 16-month-old to paddle and play. Grab a beachside palapa and relax.

👶 Shallow Calm Water 🌊 Super Relaxed 🚕 ~$5–8 taxi
11:30 AM – 1:00 PM

🌮 Lunch at a Mahahual Beach Bar

Enjoy fresh fish tacos, guacamole, and fresh coconut agua fresca at one of the laid-back beach restaurants along the Malecon (boardwalk). Most places are relaxed and toddler-friendly. Great time for a nap in the stroller!

🌮 Fish Tacos 😴 Stroller Nap Time 🥥 Chill Vibes
1:00 – 2:30 PM

🌴 Back to Pier – Pool & Shopping

Head back to the pier area. Let your toddler enjoy the free pier pool while you browse the artisan shops. Pick up hammocks, Mayan masks, or vanilla extract as souvenirs. Cool and shaded walkways make this easy.

🏊 Free Pier Pool 🛍️ Souvenir Shopping
2:30 – 3:30 PM

⛵ Return to Ship

Be back at the ship at least 60–90 min before departure. Rinse off, change your toddler, and settle in. Head to the ship's pool deck for a refreshing dip before dinner!

⚠️ Don't miss the ship!

💡 Costa Maya Tips with a Toddler

  • 🌞Apply reef-safe sunscreen every 90 min — the Mexican sun is intense in April!
  • 🧴Bring a portable shade tent or beach umbrella — the pier has limited shade on the beach.
  • 💵USD is widely accepted at the pier. Carry small bills ($1–$5) for tips and snacks.
  • 🚶The pier area is fully walkable & stroller-friendly — no need for an excursion!
  • 🦺Skip the cenotes & ruins tours with a 16-month-old — save those for the next trip!
  • 🧊Stay hydrated! Bring extra water bottles for your toddler.

🎒 Pack for Costa Maya

🩱 Baby swimsuit + swim diaper
☀️ Reef-safe SPF 50 sunscreen
👒 Wide-brim sun hat (toddler)
🧴 Baby rash guard (UV top)
🍌 Toddler snacks (banana, crackers)
💧 2× large water bottles
👟 Sandals (easy on/off for beach)
🧸 1 comfort toy
🪣 Small sand bucket & shovel
🩹 Mini first aid kit

Roatán, Honduras

Stunning coral reef island • Lush & tropical • Calm Caribbean beaches

🌤️ ~29°C / 85°F  ·  Tropical & Breezy
7:00 – 8:00 AM

🍳 Breakfast on the Ship

Same strategy as Costa Maya — fill up on the ship. Pack a snack bag for the toddler and pre-apply sunscreen before you head off.

🚢 Start Strong 🥞 Ship Breakfast
8:30 – 10:00 AM

🌺 West Bay Beach

West Bay Beach is consistently rated one of the most beautiful beaches in the Caribbean. The water is impossibly clear and very calm — like a natural bathtub for your toddler. Arrive early to claim a shaded palapa and enjoy the beach before the crowds. A taxi water shuttle (~$5–10) or a short cab ride gets you there.

👶 Calm Crystal Water 🏖️ World-Class Beach 🌴 Arrive Early!
10:00 – 11:00 AM

🐠 Snorkel Viewing (Adults Take Turns!)

West Bay sits on one of the best coral reefs in the Western Hemisphere. One parent can snorkel steps from the shore while the other stays in the shallows with the toddler, then swap. The reef starts just 10–15 meters from shore — no boat needed!

🤿 Take Turns Snorkeling 👶 Shallow play for baby 🐠 Incredible Marine Life
11:00 AM – 12:30 PM

🥘 Lunch at a Beachfront Restaurant

West Bay has wonderful open-air restaurants right on the sand. Try Sundowner's or Bite on the Beach for fresh ceviche, grilled fish, and tropical drinks. Most are relaxed and welcoming to families with small children. Perfect time for a toddler nap under the palapa! 😴

🐟 Fresh Ceviche 😴 Post-swim Nap 🍹 Tropical Drinks
12:30 – 2:00 PM

🦜 Gumbalimba Park (Optional)

If your toddler is energized after lunch, Gumbalimba Park is a short ride away. It has tropical gardens, macaws, monkeys, and a beach — all in one. It's toddler-safe, shaded, and stroller-friendly along most paths. The macaws landing on shoulders is a magical memory! Entry: ~$25–35/adult.

🦜 Macaws & Monkeys! 🌿 Shaded Jungle Walk 📸 Amazing Photos
2:00 – 3:30 PM

🛍️ Mahogany Bay Pier / Back to Ship

Head back toward the ship via Mahogany Bay — the pier area has a zipline over the water (one parent could try while the other chills with the toddler!), artisan shops, and a beautiful beach. Browse local crafts — Honduran coffee, wooden carvings, and jewelry make great gifts.

🛍️ Local Crafts 🤸 Pier Zipline (Adults!) ⚠️ Back before all-aboard!
3:30 – 4:00 PM

⛵ Board the Ship

Head back on board, rinse off, and enjoy a golden Caribbean sunset from the deck with your little one. A perfect end to a magical Roatán day! 🌅

🌅 Sunset on Deck

💡 Roatán Tips with a Toddler

  • 🚕Agree on taxi fares before you get in — always negotiate. $10–15 from pier to West Bay is reasonable.
  • 🤿Bring your own baby swim vest / floatie if your toddler loves the water — rentals for infants are rare.
  • 🌊West Bay Beach has no harsh waves — it's extremely safe for toddlers who love water.
  • 🦟Pack insect repellent — the jungle areas have mosquitoes, especially near Gumbalimba.
  • 📱Download offline maps before leaving the ship — roaming data can be expensive in Honduras.
  • 💵USD is accepted everywhere. Lempiras are the local currency but rarely needed by tourists.

🎒 Pack for Roatán

🩱 Baby swimsuit + swim diaper
☀️ Reef-safe SPF 50 sunscreen
🤿 Snorkel gear (adults)
🦺 Toddler swim vest / floatie
🧴 Baby rash guard + hat
🦟 Baby-safe insect repellent
🍌 Toddler snacks & pouches
💧 Reusable water bottles
📷 Waterproof phone case
🩹 Mini first aid kit
