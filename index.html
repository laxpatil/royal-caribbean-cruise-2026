🏖️ Choose Your Beach — 3 Options

🆓 Free

🏊 Option A — Pier Pool & Splash Area

$0 · Right at the cruise pier

The Costa Maya port complex has a beautiful free pool, splash area, and sandy beach — all steps from the ship. Shaded palapas, Mayan ruins replicas for photos, and an open-air market. Zero effort, perfect for toddlers.

  • ✅ Completely free
  • ✅ Steps from the ship
  • ✅ Pool + splash area
  • ✅ Shaded areas
  • ✅ Easiest with a toddler
⭐ Our Pick

🌴 Option B — Mahahual Beach (Town)

~$5–8 taxi + $15–20 palapa rental + food

A short taxi ride south to the charming beach town of Mahahual. Think crystal-clear calm water, colorful Malecón boardwalk, beachfront restaurants with your toes in the sand. Much more authentic & beautiful than the pier. The water stays knee-deep for 30+ feet — toddler heaven!

  • ✅ Authentic beach town vibe
  • ✅ Ultra-calm, shallow water
  • ✅ Beachfront restaurants
  • ✅ Walkable Malecón boardwalk
  • ✅ Great for exploring + food
🏖️ Beach Club

🥂 Option C — Maya Chan Beach Club

~$65/adult all-inclusive · Kids under 5 free · Pre-book required

The highest-rated beach club near Costa Maya. Private beach with all-inclusive food, open bar, kayaks & snorkeling. Limited to ~100 guests — peaceful and uncrowded. Includes round-trip transport from pier. Book early — sells out fast!

  • ✅ All-inclusive food & drinks
  • ✅ Transport included
  • ✅ Max 100 guests
  • ✅ Kayaks + snorkeling
  • ✅ Kids under 5 free!
  • ⚠️ Must pre-book at mayachan.com

🤿 Snorkeling — Take Turns!

⭐ Best Value

🐠 Shore Snorkel at Mahahual Reef

Free (bring gear) or rent ~$10–15

Walk into the water from Mahahual beach and snorkel along the Mesoamerican Barrier Reef — the 2nd largest reef in the world! Colorful fish, sea fans, and occasionally sea turtles and rays. One parent snorkels while the other plays with the toddler in the shallows.

  • ✅ 2nd largest reef in the world!
  • ✅ Free with own gear
  • ✅ Sea turtles possible
  • ✅ Steps from the beach
🚤 Excursion

🐢 Banco Chinchorro Snorkel Tour

~$80–120/adult · Full day trip · 5–6 hours

A boat trip to the Chinchorro Atoll — a remote, pristine coral atoll with massive coral heads, nurse sharks, and incredible visibility. This is a bucket-list snorkel. However, it's a long day — better for a future trip without the toddler.

  • ✅ World-class pristine reef
  • ✅ Nurse sharks & large coral
  • ⚠️ Very long day (5–6 hrs)
  • ⚠️ Not ideal with a toddler
  • 💡 Save for next trip!

Consistently ranked #1 beach in Honduras and top 10 in the Caribbean. Powdery white sand, impossibly clear calm water, and a world-class reef just 15 meters from shore. Multiple beachfront restaurants. The water is like a warm bathtub — your toddler will be in heaven.

The easiest option — a small but pretty beach right at the Mahogany Bay pier. Free lounge chairs, a chairlift over the water, and shops. Water is calm and clear. Not as stunning as West Bay, but zero taxi hassle with a toddler.

  • ✅ Steps from the ship

🤿 Snorkeling — Roatán's Crown Jewel!

⭐ Must-Do

🐠 Shore Snorkel at West Bay Reef

Free (bring gear) or rent ~$10–15

The Mesoamerican Barrier Reef starts just 10–15 meters from the sand at West Bay. Simply walk in and you're floating over vibrant coral, parrotfish, angelfish, sea fans, and sea turtles. One of the best shore-accessible reefs on Earth. Take turns — one parent snorkels while the other plays with the toddler!

  • ✅ World-class reef from shore!
  • ✅ Sea turtles common
  • ✅ Free with own gear
  • ✅ 15 meters from sand
  • ✅ Warm, clear water
🚤 Excursion

🐢 Guided Reef & Shipwreck Tour

~$45–65/adult · ~2 hours · Equipment included

Boat to deeper reef sections and a sunken shipwreck with nurse sharks, eagle rays, and massive coral formations. Incredible visibility (100+ feet!). One parent goes on the tour while the other stays at the beach — then swap on the next departure.

  • ✅ Shipwreck snorkeling!
  • ✅ Nurse sharks & rays
  • ✅ 100+ ft visibility
  • ✅ Equipment included
  • ✅ Multiple departures
🏛️ Park

🐬 Gumbalimba Park + Snorkel

~$25–35/adult entry · Kids under 4 free

Snorkeling + tropical animals + Mayan culture in one place. The park has its own beach for snorkeling, plus macaws, capuchin monkeys, iguanas, botanical gardens, and a hanging bridge walk. Your toddler will be mesmerized by the birds and monkeys! 🦜

  • ✅ Snorkel + animals + nature
  • ✅ Macaws land on your shoulder!
  • ✅ Capuchin monkeys
  • ✅ Stroller-friendly paths
  • ✅ All-in-one experience
  • 📸 Incredible family photos

💡 CocoCay Tips with a Toddler

▼
  • 🚤CocoCay is a tender port — you take a small boat from the ship. Strollers are allowed but fold yours before boarding the tender.
  • ⏰Get in line early (7:30 AM) — first tenders mean best beach spots and no crowds.
  • 💧Bring refillable water bottles — free water stations are spread across the island.
  • 🏊The Oasis Lagoon freshwater pool is massive and has a shallow end for toddlers.
  • 👶There are no changing facilities with baby tables — bring a portable changing pad in your bag.
  • 💵Everything is charged to your SeaPass card — no cash needed on the island.
  • 📶Ship WiFi doesn't work on the island — download offline entertainment for the toddler just in case.
  • 🧴Reef-safe sunscreen only! The Bahamas requires it to protect coral reefs.

💡 Cozumel Tips with a Toddler

▼
  • 🚢Cozumel is a dock port — the ship pulls right up. No tender! Easy on/off with a stroller.
  • 💵Mexican pesos get better prices at local shops, but USD is accepted everywhere. ATMs at the port give pesos.
  • 🚕Taxis are not metered — always agree on the fare before getting in. Port to south hotel zone: ~$10–15.
  • 🤿El Cielo must be pre-booked — search "Cozumel El Cielo snorkel tour" on Viator or local operators.
  • 👟Water shoes are essential for rocky east side beaches and Money Bar. Bring them!
  • 🦺Skip the east side of the island with a toddler — rough surf and no lifeguards.
  • 🌮Try a marquesita (crispy crepe with Nutella, cheese & fruit) — the Yucatán's iconic street snack!
  • 🧴All beach parks in Cozumel require reef-safe sunscreen — no oxybenzone!
